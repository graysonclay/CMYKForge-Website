The idea

Color planned before the first layer prints

Most image-based color printing is trial and error: loaded filaments, slicer behavior, translucency, and calibration all change the result after you hit print.

CMYKForge moves those decisions to the start. It analyzes your image, plans a CMYK-style material strategy around your actual filaments, generates a color-aware model, and exports slicer-ready files.

  • Start from a photo, artwork, or logo
  • Plan cyan, magenta, yellow, black + optional white
  • Generate layered, color-aware geometry
  • Export 3MF for real slicer workflows

A limited set of filaments becomes a full spectrum of color — planned, previewed, and prepared before the first layer prints.

Color separation
01 — Channels

Start with separated color.

CMYKForge reads your image and splits it into cyan, magenta, yellow, and key channels — the raw ingredients of a wider printable color range.

02 — Alignment

Matched to your filaments.

Each channel is planned around the real materials you’ve loaded, using their transmission distance and hex values.

03 — Print

One color-aware result.

The channels converge into a single color-aware model, ready for multi-material FDM printing.

AI is optional

AI Is Optional

CMYKForge Standard delivers the core local workflow without requiring local or cloud AI. Future editions may add optional AI assistance, but image preparation, color planning, model generation, previewing, and export remain part of the independent CMYKForge foundation.

  • Standard stays non-AI

    The local-first version does not include cloud AI or local AI by default.

  • Core tools remain local

    Image analysis, color planning, model generation, previewing, and export stay on your machine.

  • Optional AI may come later

    Future AI editions may be offered separately for extra guidance.
